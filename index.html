Opioid Safety in the VA and FDA


This work is part of an ongoing research collaboration between Stanford and the VA and FDA. The VA provided patient level data from 2016, 2017, and 2018 including information on comorbidities, demographics, mental health, and health outcomes. The VA and FDA are interested in how the opioid crisis has affected the VA patient population.

STORM is a clinical decision support tool which among other things provides a risk score associated with how likely a patient is to abuse opioids. Clinical decision support tools such as STORM must be utilized prior to prescribing opioids in the VA.

Behind this risk score is a multivariate logistic regression. One goal of the research is to develop a framework for auditing such predictive models, to see how they are performing for different demographic groups. Using the provided data, we reconstructed this model, and have looked at false negative parity and calibration across race and gender. Currently we are looking in to additional measures, such as multiaccuracy. As part of this I have developed a binary classification model pipeline in Python using sklearn and pandas which has been passed along to the VA.

Additionally, decision makers in the VA are interested in trends in their patient population. I have created interactive data visualizations using d3, HTML, and CSS which look into patient demographics and adverse health outcomes.

I began working on this project in July 2019 as part of Stanford's Data Science for Social Good research fellowship. I am now continuing this research as a graduate research assistant working with Suzanne Tamang in the Biomedical Data Science department.

Publication of this research is currently pending.

Portfolio Management with Deep Reinforcement Learning


In this work I utilize state of the art reinforcement learning methods to manage my own financial assets. Trades are executed once a day to redistribute a portfolio of stocks within the S&P500 based on the algorithmically derived policy.

I took on this work to research state of the art deep reinforcement learning methods, implement deep neural networks for my own benefit, and become more financially literate.
